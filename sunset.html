Sunset Park!

Sunset Park is a neighborhood in the southwestern part of Brooklyn. Predominantly an Asian and Hispanic neighborhood, Sunset Park is where I was born and raised. I have lots of love for my neighborhood and will highlight some of my favorite things to do in Sunset Park!


Visit the Neighborhood Park

First on the lists of "musts" in Sunset Park is to visit the park the community is named after. Located between 41st and 44th streets and 5th and 7th avenues, Sunset Park contains a playground, recreation center, and pool. Perhaps most notably, Sunset Park features hills with great views of the Manhattan skyline. You can even see the statue of liberty from here! This park is easily one of the highlights of the neighborhood and must-visit if you are in the area.


Shop on 5th Avenue

After visting the park, take a trip down 5th avenue where the streets are lined with locally owned retail stores. Due to the cultural mix of the neighborhood, you will be able to find many authentic Asian and Hispanic restaurants and shops. While there are certainly some neighborhood favorites, you can walk into any restaurant or shop and can almost guarantee you will have a great experience!


Street Tacos

While on 5th avenue, make sure you make a stop at one of the various taco trucks. There are several around the neighborhood and all of them are fantastic! My personal favorite is "Tacos El Bronco" which was featured in a New York Times article. All of these trucks have authentic offerings and are at a very affordable price. These trucks can be found all along 5th avenue.


Grab Some Conchas

Because of the large hispanic population in the neighborhood, Sunset Park is filled with bakeries serving conchas, a Mexican sweet bread. These breads are incredibly tasty and also really cheap! My favorite place to get them is "Mi Mexico Pequeno" on 45th street and 5th avenue, but conchas can be found on almost every block.


Visit Greenwood Cemetery

While not everyone's cup of tea, Greenwood Cemetery is a national historic landmark and its main entrance is located in Sunset Park! This cemetery is massive at 478 acres and has many notable burials. Generally, it is a very peaceful place to walk and even offers scenic views. A noteworthy place to visit within the cemetery is "Battle Hill," named after a battle that took place here during the American Revolution. Battle Hill is also the highest natural point in Brooklyn.
