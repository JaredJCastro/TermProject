Sights to See

There is so much to see in New York! While you can see incredible things almost anywhere in the city, below are some places you should absolutely make the time to see if you happen to be in the city.


The Highline

Located on the Upper West side, the highline is an "elevated" public park that stretches almost a mile and a half long. This is a unique concept as the park goes over the neighborhood its in, giving you unique views of the city. There are also different monuments on the highline that are all worth seeing.

Brooklyn Bridge

First completed in 1883, the Brooklyn Bridge became the first roadway connecting Brooklyn and Manhattan. This bridge is one of the most iconic places to walk in New York because of the stunning views of the city you get as you trek along the wooden boards. The city has also just recently constructed a bike path on the bridge which makes it a great spot to bike! The bridge takes about 30 minutes to walk across.

Greenwood Cemetery

Greenwood Cemetery is a national historic landmark and its main entrance is located in Sunset Park! This cemetery is massive at 478 acres and has many notable burials. Generally, it is a very peaceful place to walk and even offers scenic views. A noteworthy place to visit within the cemetery is "Battle Hill," named after a battle that took place here during the American Revolution. Battle Hill is also the highest natural point in Brooklyn.
