The Best Dessert Spots in New York

New York City is known to have fantastic food all over, and the same can be said about their dessert offerings. New York is home to some famous dessert spots and I will be highlighting some of my favorites! It should be stated that, just like with restaurants in New York, you will likely be satisfied no matter where you go, but these are some that are must-visits.


Junior's Cheesecake

Founded in 1950, Junior's is known as the home of New York's best cheesecake. Although they have several locations across the city, the landmark location is located in Brooklyn right off the DeKalb Avenue train station. Trying their cheesecake is a must, but they also have other fantastic offerings. My favorites are the plain and the brownie explosion cheesecakes.


The Cannoli King

Founded in 1973, Caffe Palermo has been dubbed the "Cannoli King" as they have won national awards for their incredible cannolis. They also offer a wide range of other options, all of which are fantastic. Their various cake options are all worth trying, but their cannolis are their bread-and-butter. It is worth noting however that Caffe Palermo is very pricey, but their offerings are worth the try!


A La Mode Shoppe

If you're craving ice cream, A La Mode Shoppe is the place to go. Located in the Upper East Side, A La Mode has (in my opinion) some of the best ice cream in New York. Although their flavor options aren't as wide as some other places, their quality is second to none. Their ice cream is also allergy free which is a nice bonus.


Mia's Bakery

Located in Cobble Hill, Mia's bakery has a wide assortment of premium baked goods. Although they don't have anything they specialize in like other spots on this list, all of products are great. With how many options they have, it's hard to leave this bakery with just one item! The bakery itself also has a nice cozy atmosphere inside.


Various Bakeries in Sunset Park

As mentioned on the Sunset Park page, stopping by any of the various bakeries in Sunset Park for conchas is a must. Conchas can be tough to find outside of hispanic communites so they are worth going out of your way to try. They are also very cheap and most bakeries in Sunset Park make them well!
